Evidence Framework

Strictly WHO-Aligned Standards

Every recommendation, threshold, and screening trigger is anchored to authoritative WHO governing documents. No independent overrides — ever.

⚖️

Adult BMI Classification

BMI thresholds follow WHO TRS 894 standards. Underweight (<18.5), normal, overweight (25–29.9), and obese (≥30) classifications trigger tiered advisory responses.

📖 WHO TRS 894
👶

Pediatric Growth Standards

Children 5–19 years are monitored against WHO z-score growth references (2006/2007). Alerts are issued at z-score thresholds with consultation prompts for severe deviations.

📖 WHO CGS 2006 · 2007
🥗

Energy & Macronutrients

Calorie and protein requirements follow the WHO/FAO/UNU Expert Consultation (2004) and TRS 935. Protein below 0.8g/kg/day for 7+ days triggers an advisory flag.

📖 WHO/FAO/UNU 2004
🏃

Physical Activity Guidelines

Activity recommendations for all life stages follow the WHO Guidelines on Physical Activity and Sedentary Behaviour (2020). Sedentary patterns trigger gentle intervention prompts.

📖 WHO GPASB 2020
🫀

Fats & Sugars Guidance

Sugar and fat recommendations align with FAO/WHO Expert Consultation on Fats (2010) and the WHO Guideline on Sugars Intake (2015). Sugar-sweetened beverage reduction is a core streak goal.

📖 WHO Sugars 2015
💊

Micronutrient Requirements

Vitamin and mineral benchmarks follow WHO/FAO Vitamin and Mineral Requirements in Human Nutrition (2nd Ed) with life-stage-specific adjustments for pregnancy, lactation, and older adults.

📖 WHO/FAO VMR 2nd Ed.
